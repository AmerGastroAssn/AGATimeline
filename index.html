The Good News: 2015 Revaluation of Colonoscopy codes delayed

Timeline to Success

  • Feb. 13, 2014

    GI society CPT and RUC advisors and staff meet with CMS to discuss the upper GI endoscopy values.


    In February, GI societies hold briefing on Capitol Hill — Progress and Challenges in Reducing Colorectal Cancer, which launches the AGA, ACG, ASGE Value of Colonoscopy campaign.

  • April 8, 2014

    The ranking Democratic members of the House Ways and Means and Energy and Commerce Committees send a letter to CMS.


    “ â€¦ lack of transparency at a crucial phase of policy development robs health care providers and their patients the opportunity to fairly and meaningfully voice their approval or concern.”


  • April 9, 2014

    GI society CPT and RUC advisors and staff meet with CMS to discuss time and intensity of upper GI endoscopy procedures and colonoscopy.

  • April 17, 2014

    The GI societies work with Rep. Bill Cassidy and 47 U.S. House members to send a letter to Administrator Tavenner.


    “Depriving health care professionals the opportunity to contribute their unique expertise in assessing the value of services is counter to the intent of federal rulemaking.”

  • May 22, 2014

    AGA, ACG and ASGE presidents meet with Administrator Tavenner to discuss reimbursement system and value of colonoscopy.

  • June 6, 2014

    GI society presidents write to Administrator Tavenner.


    “In order to succeed in this battle against colorectal cancer, it is necessary that CMS consider the life- and cost-saving roles that colonoscopy serves in Medicare patients when reviewing reimbursement.”

  • June 13, 2014

    The GI societies work with 12 Senators to send letter to CMS Administrator Marilyn Tavenner.


    “Providers and stakeholders need to be afforded adequate time to review and comment on changes to their reimbursement rates.”

  • Aug. 18, 2014

    GI society CPT and RUC advisors and staff meet with CMS to discuss the valuation of colonoscopy and lower GI endoscopy procedures.


    In the month of August, more than 450 gastroenterologists write to CMS about the value of colonoscopy and the need for a transparent rate–setting process

  • Sept. 2, 2014

    GI societies submit 27 pages of substantive comments on the 2015 proposed rule of Medicare physician reimbursement.


    “In fairness, we urge CMS to delay valuing the lower endoscopy codes and finalizing the values for the upper endoscopy codes for one year so they can benefit from transparency in the rate setting process.”

  • Sept. 30, 2014

    GI society CPT and RUC advisors and staff meet with CMS at their request to answer questions about lower GI endoscopy procedures.

  • Oct. 31, 2014

    CMS releases 2015 Medicare Physician Fee Schedule final rule, which delays revaluation of lower GI endoscopy codes until a transparent process is in place.


    Average savings of delay: Estimated at $21.50 for most commonly performed lower GI Procedures, based on current colonoscopy reimbursement.*


    *to determine approxmate cost savings, multiply $21.50 by the number of colonoscopy screening, biopsy and polypectomy procedures.
